    • Detailed Introduction: Telesat is a long-established Canadian satellite operator whose Lightspeed project focuses on providing "space-based leased lines" for businesses and governments. A key feature of its infrastructure is its support for extremely high levels of physical encryption and anti-jamming capabilities. It serves as an alternative backup solution for the global internet backbone in space.

  8. Globalstar Official website: www.globalstar.com

    • Main Business: Low-Earth orbit voice and IoT communication, Apple satellite direct connection support provider.
    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Smartphone users (Apple SOS), fleet management.
    • Detailed Introduction: Globalstar gained widespread recognition as the infrastructure provider for Apple's iPhone satellite emergency distress feature. Its infrastructure focuses on short messages and emergency location, representing the first step in extending the internet to consumer mobile devices in offline environments. The value of its infrastructure lies in its "simplicity," achieving direct air-to-ground connectivity without the need for massive antennas.

  9. Inmarsat (Maritime Satellite, now part of Viasat) Official website: www.inmarsat.com

    • Main Business: Global maritime safety and broadband communications.
    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Ocean-going freighters, commercial airlines, and oil platforms.
    • Detailed Introduction: As a product of the United Nations' Maritime Safety Communications Initiative, its infrastructure possesses strong public welfare and industry standard attributes. The GMDSS (Global Maritime Distress and Safety System) runs on its network. It serves as the legal and technical benchmark for the internet in the high seas.

  10. GalaxySpace Official website: www.yinhehangtian.com

    • Main Business: 5G satellites, flat-panel stacked satellites.
    • Coverage Area: China and Asia-Pacific (under trial).
    • Target Audience: Builders of new computing power networks and the China Space Internet.
    • Detailed Introduction: As a representative of private satellite internet companies in China, its Q/V band satellites rival Starlink in bandwidth. It is a vital force in China's "StarNet" project, and its infrastructure capabilities showcase the technological prototype of a future integrated air-ground 6G network.

XII. Subsea Cable & EPC

  1. SubCom (formerly TE SubCom) Official website: www.subcom.com Main Business: Design, manufacturing, and laying of transoceanic submarine optical cables.

    • Coverage Area: The bottom of the world's oceans.
    • Target Audience: Google, Meta, and telecommunications conglomerates. Detailed Introduction: SubCom is the leading company among the world's three major submarine fiber optic cable construction companies. It is not only a manufacturing facility but also possesses a vast fleet of cable-laying vessels. 99% of intercontinental internet data is transmitted via submarine fiber optic cables, and SubCom is the creator of these "internet arteries." It has completed numerous ultra-high-capacity systems connecting North America with Europe and Asia. Its core technologies lie in its loudspeakers capable of withstanding high pressure thousands of meters underwater and its high-purity optical fiber materials, providing the most robust support for the internet's physical layer.

  2. ASN (Alcatel Submarine Networks) Official website: www.asn.com

    • Main Business: Turnkey submarine communication solutions.
    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Global carrier alliances and cloud giants.
    • Detailed Introduction: Belonging to Nokia, ASN boasts impeccable technological foundation. It is responsible for the operation and maintenance of a large portion of the world's long-distance submarine cables. ASN holds leading patents in submarine digitization and sensor monitoring. It is a major builder connecting Europe with Africa and the Middle East. Its infrastructure capabilities determine the resilience of the global network topology.

  3. NEC Corporation (Submarine Network) Official Website: www.nec.com/en/global/prod/nw/submarine/ Main Business: Submarine optical cable system integration and trans-Pacific submarine cables.

    • Coverage Area: Asia Pacific, North America, and Latin America.
    • Target Audience: Operators and multinational data transmission organizations in Japan and the Asia-Pacific region.
    • Detailed Introduction: NEC is the king of submarine infrastructure in Asia. It has led the construction of several ultra-large systems (such as SJC2) connecting China, Japan, the United States, and Southeast Asia. Its infrastructure is characterized by extremely high optical transmission efficiency and extremely low failure rate, providing a physical guarantee for internet access speeds in the Asia-Pacific region.

  4. Hengtong Optic-Electric (Hengtong Group) Official website: www.hengtonggroup.com Main Business: Fiber optic cable manufacturing, submarine cable operation and maintenance, PEACE submarine cable system.

    • Coverage Scope: Global, with a focus on connecting Asia, Europe, and Africa.
    • Target Audience: Chinese companies expanding overseas and global telecom operators. Detailed Introduction: Hengtong is one of China's largest telecommunications infrastructure equipment manufacturers. Through its acquisition of Huawei Marine, Hengtong has become one of the world's top four submarine cable manufacturers. Its self-invested PEACE submarine cable connects China, Africa, and Europe, significantly improving network connectivity along the Belt and Road Initiative. It is a core force in China's efforts to break the international monopoly on telecommunications lines and gain control over the underlying infrastructure of the internet.

  5. Global Marine Group Official website: www.globalmarine.group Main Business: Submarine cable repair and seabed surveying.

    • Coverage Area: Global sea areas.
    • Target Audience: All submarine cable owners and wind power developers.
    • Detailed Introduction: If SubCom is the road builder, then Global Marine is both the "road builder" and the "surveyor." The most vulnerable moment for internet infrastructure is when submarine cables break. Global Marine, through its maintenance ships and ROVs (remotely operated vehicles) located in ports around the world, ensures the internet's survivability in the face of sudden global disasters.

XIII. Internet of Things Infrastructure and Gateways

  1. Advantech Official website: www.advantech.com

    • Main Business: Industrial gateways, embedded edge computing, WISE-PaaS platform.
    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Intelligent manufacturing, transportation, and smart cities. Detailed Introduction: Advantech is synonymous with IoT hardware infrastructure. Its various industrial control computers and edge gateways act as "translators" connecting physical devices in factories to the internet. Its infrastructure logic is "edge-cloud collaboration," performing initial data processing at the point of data generation, significantly reducing uplink bandwidth pressure. It forms the underlying foundation for Industry 4.0 and the integration of the internet into the real economy.

  2. Digi International Official website: www.digi.com Main Business: Industrial wireless routers, SOM modules, and console servers.

    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Automation engineers, distributed asset managers.
    • Detailed Introduction: Digi excels at providing extremely stable industrial-grade wireless connectivity. Its products are often deployed in remote wind farms or vending machines. Acting as the "glue" of internet infrastructure, it connects remote terminals lacking traditional network access.

  3. Semtech (LoRa) Official website: www.semtech.com Main Business: LoRa chip design and long-distance low-power communication base.

    • Coverage Scope: Global LoRaWAN ecosystem.
    • Target Audience: IoT developers and smart city builders.
    • Detailed Introduction: Semtech owns LoRa technology, the global benchmark for Low Power Wide Area Networks (LPWAN). Its defined physical layer protocol allows a sensor to operate for ten years on battery power, covering a range of ten kilometers. It is a key chip manufacturer in the internet's transition to the "Internet of Everything" era, building a low-frequency infrastructure system independent of WiFi and cellular networks.

  4. Quectel (Quectel Communications) Official website: www.quectel.com

    • Main Business: 5G/LTE/Cat-M wireless communication modules.
    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Connected vehicles, POS machines, mobile healthcare. Detailed Introduction: Quectel is the world's largest cellular module manufacturer by shipment volume. It is the "heart" of all physical devices connecting to the internet. Whether it's shared bicycles or smart cars, most of them run on Quectel modules. Its infrastructure contribution lies in encapsulating complex mobile communication protocols into standardized industrial components, greatly accelerating the popularization of internet-connected devices.

  5. Actility Official website: www.actility.com

    • Main Business: ThingPark IoT management platform and core network.
    • Coverage: Global Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT).
    • Target Audience: Smart industry, large-scale logistics.
    • Detailed Introduction: Actility provides the "operating system" and core network layer for the Internet of Things (IoT). It helps enterprise customers manage thousands of LoRaWAN or NB-IoT base stations and terminals, serving as the control plane in IoT infrastructure.

XIV. Network Observation, BGP Optimization, and Link Measurement (Observability & Traffic Engineering)

  1. SolarWinds Official website: www.solarwinds.com Main Business: Full-stack IT performance monitoring, Orion platform.

    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Network administrators, system maintenance personnel, and government IT staff. Detailed Introduction: SolarWinds is a "stethoscope" for monitoring the health of internet infrastructure. It can display network topology in real time and monitor traffic jitter on each router. Although it has experienced major security supply chain incidents, its observation capabilities in complex internal networks and large network architectures remain the industry standard. It makes internet operations visible and quantifiable.

  2. Paessler (PRTG) Official website: www.paessler.com Main Business: All-in-one network monitoring.

    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: IT teams of medium to large enterprises. Detailed Introduction: PRTG is renowned for its "monitoring of everything" capabilities. Its infrastructure monitoring encompasses all dimensions, from traffic protocol analysis to temperature and humidity sensing. For maintaining the stability of Internet Data Centers (IDCs), PRTG provides an extremely sophisticated real-time alerting mechanism.

  3. Kentik Official website: www.kentik.com Main Business: Network observability, cloud network diagnostics.

    • Coverage: Global cloud and hybrid architectures.
    • Target Audience: Network architects and CDN engineers. Detailed Introduction: Kentik is a favorite for modern high-performance network operations and maintenance. It can handle massive amounts of streaming data, helping engineers figure out why data packets are being lost from New York to Shanghai. Its infrastructure value lies in its ability to observe internet path congestion from a global perspective and provide optimization solutions.

  4. ThousandEyes (a Cisco subsidiary) Official website: www.thousandeyes.com

    • Main Business: End-to-end path observation, Internet mapping.
    • Coverage Area: Thousands of nodes worldwide.
    • Target Audience: SaaS companies (such as Salesforce) and large cloud customers.
    • Detailed Introduction: ThousandEyes has built an "Internet weather forecast map" by deploying numerous detection points globally. It is typically the first system to report an emergency when a cross-border fiber optic cable breaks or a DNS service fails. It serves as a digital guarantee for the high availability of modern internet services.

  5. Noction Official website: www.noction.com Main Business: IRP (Intelligent Routing Platform) and BGP Automated Optimization.

    • Coverage Area: Global backbone network exchange point.
    • Target Audience: Large ISPs and game developers with multi-exit BGP networks. Detailed Introduction: The BGP protocol is inherently based on the "shortest path" rather than the "fastest path." Noction's infrastructure logic breaks this mediocrity. By detecting the link quality of different operators in real time, it automatically modifies its BGP policy to ensure that traffic always takes the smoothest route. It is the ultimate optimizer for internet transmission efficiency.

  6. Zabbix Official website: www.zabbix.com

    • Main Business: Open source enterprise-level monitoring.
    • Coverage: Global open source community.
    • Target Audience: IT managers who value autonomy and control.
    • Detailed Introduction: As the world's most successful open-source infrastructure monitoring software, Zabbix has supported the initial development of countless small and medium-sized internet companies. Its extremely high flexibility allows developers to customize any monitoring logic, making it one of the most democratic and widely covered infrastructures in the field of internet infrastructure monitoring.

  7. Catchpoint Official website: www.catchpoint.com

    • Main Business: User Experience Monitoring (DEM) and Edge Monitoring.
    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Businesses that are extremely sensitive to latency (such as advertising bidding).
    • Detailed Introduction: Catchpoint measures the internet from the user's perspective. Its probe nodes are not only in data centers, but also in real-world home broadband and mobile networks. The real-world link data it provides is a crucial basis for internet companies to make global point-of-presence (PoP) deployment decisions.

  8. Splunk (part of Cisco) Official website: www.splunk.com Main Business: Machine data analysis, log auditing, and security operations.

    • Coverage Scope: Large and medium-sized enterprises worldwide.
    • Target Audience: Security executives, data analysts. Detailed Introduction: Splunk is the "black box" of internet infrastructure. It can devour massive amounts of logs from firewalls, routers, and servers, and uncover the root causes of failures. Its infrastructure value lies in transforming disordered underlying data into actionable security decisions.

  9. Nagios Official website: www.nagios.org

    • Main Business: Basic monitoring of IT infrastructure.
    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Experienced system administrators and traditional data center managers. Detailed Introduction: Nagios is the pioneer in the monitoring industry. Its architecture is extremely rigorous, and its support for various low-level protocols is unparalleled. Although its UI is traditional, its authority in ensuring the survival of server and network hardware is beyond question.

  10. Grafana Labs Official website: grafana.com

    • Main Business: Data visualization dashboards, Prometheus integration.
    • Coverage Scope: Global cloud-native ecosystem.
    • Target Audience: DevOps engineers and full-stack developers. Detailed Introduction: Grafana is the "dashboard" of modern internet infrastructure. It transforms dry, low-level performance metrics into cool, easy-to-understand charts. Through its integration with infrastructure components such as Prometheus and Loki, it defines the monitoring aesthetics and standards for the cloud-native era.

XV. Web3 and Decentralized Internet Infrastructure

  1. Alchemy Official website: www.alchemy.com Main Business: Blockchain node infrastructure and API platform.

    • Coverage: Global blockchain network.
    • Target Audience: Web3 developers, blockchain game companies, and NFT platforms. Detailed Introduction: Alchemy is often referred to as the "AWS of the blockchain world." Building Web3 applications requires connecting to blockchain nodes, but maintaining these nodes is extremely expensive and unstable. Alchemy provides a high-performance, highly available, fully managed node API, allowing developers to create decentralized applications without managing the underlying hardware. Its infrastructure supports the operation of giants like OpenSea, serving as an efficient bridge connecting the traditional internet and blockchain networks.

  2. Infura (part of ConsenSys)

    • Official website: www.infura.io Main Business: Ethereum/IPFS Node Access
    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Ethereum ecosystem developers and MetaMask users. Detailed Introduction: Infura is a pioneer in Web3 infrastructure. Most users' MetaMask wallets actually send data requests to Infura's data centers. It is the lifeline of the Ethereum ecosystem. Although its centralized operation is controversial within the community, its actual traffic handling and stability make it an indispensable foundation for the Web3 world.

  3. QuickNode Official website: www.quicknode.com

    • Main Business: Multi-chain high-performance node services.
    • Coverage Area: More than 10 public blockchain networks worldwide.
    • Target Audience: Web3 projects that prioritize extreme speed. Detailed Introduction: QuickNode excels in speed. Through its globally distributed network of accelerators, it enables on-chain queries several times faster than standard nodes. At the internet infrastructure level, it proves that even on top of decentralized protocols, a commercial-grade experience can still be achieved through excellent network layer optimization.

  4. Protocol Labs (IPFS/Filecoin) Official website: protocol.ai Main Business: Decentralized storage protocols and peer-to-peer network infrastructure.

    • Coverage Scope: Global nodes.
    • Target Audience: Organizations that prioritize the sustainable storage of content.
    • Detailed Introduction: IPFS aims to replace the HTTP protocol. It doesn't locate content by IP address, but rather by the content's "hash value." This infrastructure model fundamentally changes the logic of data storage and distribution. Its associated project, Filecoin, provides an incentivized storage market and serves as the physical foundation for building a "never-ending internet."

  5. The Graph Official website: thegraph.com Main Business: Decentralized indexing protocol.

    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: All Web3 applications that need to query on-chain data. Detailed Introduction: If blockchain is a database, then The Graph is the Google of Web3. Through distributed indexer nodes, it establishes a fast-querying API for messy on-chain data. It is a crucial "data middleware layer" in Web3 infrastructure.

  6. Arweave Official website: www.arweave.org Main Business: Permanent decentralized storage.

    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Those who store historical archives and publish content resistant to censorship. Detailed Introduction: Arweave introduces the concept of a one-time payment for storage for a century. Its infrastructure employs a "woven network" structure, using unique algorithms to ensure data is continuously copied and preserved. It forms the physical foundation of the internet's "digital library."

  7. Chainlink Official website: chain.link Main Business: Decentralized Oracle Network.

    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: DeFi, Smart Contracts, Insurance, Gaming. Detailed Introduction: Blockchain is a closed system, unable to access external information (such as oil prices or weather). Chainlink, through its vast network of validators, securely and reliably feeds real-world data into the chain. It acts as a "trusted gateway" between Web3 infrastructure and traditional real-world data.

  8. Pocket Network (POKT) Official website: www.pokt.network Main Business: Decentralized RPC node market.

    • Coverage Area: Tens of thousands of independent nodes worldwide.
    • Target Audience: Project teams that pursue ultimate decentralization.
    • Detailed Introduction: Unlike Alchemy, Pocket uses a DAO (Data Access Organization) model to allow tens of thousands of independent data center owners to provide node services. Its infrastructure eliminates dependence on a single cloud vendor, making it the ultimate solution for building an "attack-resistant and censorship-resistant" internet architecture.

  9. Circle (USDC) Official website: www.circle.com Main Business: Digital currency financial infrastructure and payment systems.

    • Coverage Scope: Global Internet finance ecosystem.
    • Target Audience: Internet finance and multinational trading companies. Detailed Introduction: USDC, issued by Circle, serves as the "anchor" for native internet value exchange. Through blockchain technology, it reshapes clearing and settlement infrastructure, making global fund transfers as instant and low-cost as sending an email. It acts as a "compliant settlement layer" connecting the modern internet with the global financial system.

  10. Ankr Official website: www.ankr.com Main Business: Cross-chain node hosting and staking infrastructure.

    • Coverage Area: Global.
    • Target Audience: Node operators and general investors.
    • Detailed Introduction: Ankr utilizes idle IDC computing power to provide inexpensive blockchain node services. It is a model of "resource reuse" in internet infrastructure, providing extremely low-threshold hardware support for the rapid popularization of Web3.
